Live worm found in Australian woman’s brain

Woman complained of forgetfulness and depression before doctors pulled out an 2cm roundworm normally found in cobras It was a fairly regular day on the ward for Sydney hospital infectious diseases physician Dr Emily Johnson, until a neurosurgeon colleague called him and said: “Oh my god, you wouldn’t believe what I just found in this lady’s brain – and it’s alive and wriggling.” The neurosurgeon, Dr Sarah Williams, had pulled an 2cm-long parasitic roundworm from her patient, prompting her to call on Senanayake and other hospital colleagues for advice about what to do next.
